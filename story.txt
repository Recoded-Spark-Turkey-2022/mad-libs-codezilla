It was a pleasure[n] to burn. It was a special[a] pleasure to see things[n] eaten, to see[v] things blackened and changed. With the brass nozzle in his fists, with this great python[n] spitting its venomous kerosene[n] upon the world, the blood pounded in his head[n] and his hands were the hands of some amazing conductor[n] playing all the symphonies of blazing and burning to bring down the tatters and charcoal ruins of history. With his symbolic[a] helmet numbered 451 on his stolid[a] head, and his eyes all orange flame with the thought of what came[v] next, he flicked[v] the igniter and the house jumped up in a gorging fire that burned the evening sky red[n] and yellow and black. He strode[v] in a swarm of fireflies.
